Recent Advances in Time Series Foundation Models
Have We Reached the 'BERT Moment'? (BERTS)

Saturday Dec. 6th 2025, San Diego


Following the remarkable success of foundation models in NLP and computer vision, over 20 new FMs for time series analysis were proposed in the past year. Despite their promise and encouraging results, multiple studies reported that carefully designed lightweight supervised baselines can be on par with TSFMs (FMs outperforming comprehensively supervised baselines is referred to as the `BERT Moment' in NLP, which explains the title of our workshop. In real-world applications, the performance of TSFMs remains subpar without full fine-tuning. A recent study also showed that a tabular FM can rival TSFMs in terms of time series forecasting while its training is agnostic to time series. Finally, even the recently proposed benchmarks provide conflicting evidence on the performance of TSFMs. On GIFT-Eval, TSFMs outperform other methods; OpenTS benchmarks reports that on univariate datasets, simple statistical methods beat deep learning ones, while the FoundTS study shows that supervised baselines are on par with TSFMs. This highlights the need for bringing together researchers who develop TSFMs and the broader time series community to review the field's evolution and identify meaningful ways to move forward. This workshop will focus on a clear and topical problem of the contrast between the promises of TSFMs and their subpar performance compared to smaller, task-specific baselines. This discrepancy and the absence of efficient zero-shot plug-and-play TSFM models for real-world applications underscore the need to find challenging evaluation benchmarks and real-world tasks where TSFMs can truly shine.

Call for papers

  • Benchmarking Foundation Models in Time Series,
  • Scaling Laws and Efficiency in Time Series Models,
  • Evaluating Transferability and Adaptability of Foundation Models,
  • Leveraging Foundation Models of Other Modalities for Time Series,
  • Unsupervised performance estimation of TSFMs,
  • Industrial Benchmarking of Time Series Foundation Models
